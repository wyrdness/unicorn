# Sources & References

## Ancient & Classical Sources

### Greek & Roman
- Ctesias of Cnidus. (c. 400 BCE). *Indica*. (Description of one-horned wild ass in India)
- Aristotle. (c. 350 BCE). *History of Animals*. (References to one-horned animals)
- Pliny the Elder. (77 CE). *Natural History*. Book VIII. (Description of monoceros)
- Aelian. (c. 200 CE). *On the Nature of Animals*. (Various unicorn-like creatures)

## Medieval Sources

### Bestiaries
- *Physiologus* (c. 2nd-4th century CE). Early Christian bestiary with unicorn allegory.
- *Aberdeen Bestiary* (c. 12th century). Illuminated manuscript with unicorn descriptions.
- *Ashmole Bestiary* (c. 13th century). English bestiary with detailed unicorn entry.

### Medieval Texts
- Hildegard of Bingen. (12th century). *Physica*. (Medicinal properties of alicorn)
- Marco Polo. (c. 1300). *The Travels*. (Descriptions of "unicorns" - likely rhinoceros)
